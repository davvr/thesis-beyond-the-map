Electoral projection

Seat allocation
from CIS surveys

Provincial seat allocation projected from the six most recent CIS monthly barometers under the D'Hondt method. Vote intentions are aggregated across barometers using the survey-calibrated weights (PESO), then allocated to seats by province following Spain's current electoral law. Updated automatically each month. There are three measures, each answering a slightly different question:

  • Six-month aggregate: Pools the six most recent barometers into a single dataset, producing the most stable projection at the cost of some recency. This view prioritises sample size and stability.
  • Latest barometer: Uses only the most recent monthly survey, giving the freshest snapshot available but resting on a smaller provincial sample, making it more sensitive to month-to-month variation.
  • Swing projection: Starts from the actual provincial results of the July 2023 general election and adjusts them proportionally according to the national shift observed in the latest barometer. This approach anchors territorial detail in real electoral results rather than in the barometer's provincial subsamples, making it most robust in sparsely populated provinces where survey samples are thin.
